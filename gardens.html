Sunbathing Natives

The Sunbathing Natives garden illustrates successful plants and practices for brutal full sun with rocky limestone soil. Wendy Buck, the 2015 IAGT Chair, described the garden this way: “A rural Hill Country respite sits on a hill on Weston Lane. It could be outside Boerne, Blanco, or Llano, but it’s hidden in the midst of suburban Austin.”
Sunbathing Natives Website

Travis County Agricultural Ext

Gardens amaze the senses, lighten one’s heart, slow us down a bit in our busy day and ground us in the natural world. Mother Nature’s creatures have found a home in our garden: birds, butterflies, dragonflies, lizards, beneficial insects and sometimes pesky ones too. Enjoy strolling through this ADA compliant garden, a visual feast of plants in bloom, colorful foliage, herbs, and vegetables producing a bounty which we donate to the Capital Area Food Bank.
